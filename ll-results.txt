The first preliminary event for the LTD Cup was held on Wednesday, March 25th at Launch Labs Golf (www.launchlabsgolf.com) and was a great time! The event was purely recreational, with no rewards other than bragging rights at stake.

12 players competed in a 9-hole tournament on the back 9 at Pebble Beach - featuring a brutal starting hole 10 with a fairway cambered directly toward the Pacific Ocean. Kirk kicked off the tournament by immediately slicing one onto the beach, but was able to safely chip back up the cliff into play. Having everyone in the same room with a leaderboard that updated dynamically as each team finished a hole was tremendous for the trash talking capacity - Victor absolutely heard about it all night when his group was the first to show up on the leaderboard and he had carded a triple bogey 7 on the first hole. 

Don't feel too badly for Victor, because he put his tee shot on #17 to 8' 10", taking home the closest to the pin competition by narrowly edging out Vince's 9' 4"! Conway would be third on the podium with his 31' 4", and Rob Neimeyer was the only other competitor to make the green. Victor almost took home long drive honors as well, with a booming 252.9 yarder on the Par 5 14th - however Jeff Hull would edge him with a 256.2 yard drive of his own, pushing Victor to second place. John Slattery's 197.4 yards was good for third place, with Rusty being the only other player to hit the fairway on the hole.

In terms of actual scoring, Rob Neimeyer was the one making the early noise as he holed out from off the green on three of the first four holes he played! The fifth hole (#14, the long uphill par 5) brought a catastrophic double-bogey when Rob couldn't avoid the tree on the right side, bringing him back to the field. For the majority of the evening, Rob and Kevin "Fairway" Conway held the lead, with Damon Doe and Vince Cressio lurking. 

With all three groups approaching the 18th tee box, Vince had snuck into the solo lead at +4 with Kevin sitting +5 right behind. Vince immediately put his tee shot into the Pacific Ocean with a snap hook. A nice recovery capped by a chip-in from just off the green led to a bogey, bringing him into a tie with Kevin Conway at +5. Kevin had a shot, but his approach shot narrowly missed the 8-foot birdie circle, meaning we had a tie at the top!

With some help from David Gonzalez (one of the owners of Launch Labs), we set up a playoff hole on the iconic par 3 7th. Kevin drew the short straw, and was forced to play first. His tee shot was majestic and landed safely in the middle of the green, down with an automatic two-putt for par. Vince - with all 13 other people in attendance watching - had three options: Hit the green to force another playoff hole, miss the green to lose the tournament, or put it inside the 8-foot circle for a birdie and a tournament win. He stepped up, and a soft 48 degree wedge floated down, landing and spinning back to 3.2 feet for a birdie and the win!
