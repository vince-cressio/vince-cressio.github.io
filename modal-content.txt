Round 1 - Best Ball
Format: Best Ball - in our case, Four Ball Match Play - is a 2-man team event where both partners play their individual golf ball from tee to green on every hole, using their own course handicap. At the end of each hole, the player with the lowest net score on the hole (or "best ball") out of the 2-person team serves as the team's score for that hole - the higher scoring player's score is completely ignored. As an example, let's say Rusty and Ted are partners, and on the first hole Rusty pops but Ted does not. Both score gross bogey 5's on the first hole. Since Rusty pops and Ted does not, their net scores are 4 (Rusty) and 5 (Ted). Their team score on the first hole will be 4, the "best ball" of the two of them. Now this is a Match Play event, so this score will be compared against the score of the other team in the foursome. So when Conway rolls in his 35-foot birdie putt for a net 3 (you can bet your ass Conway isn't getting a pop on this hole) to save Kirk's bacon after he hit two trees and a pedestrian on three consecutive shots, Team Drawshank Redemption will take the first hole of the match to go 1-up. This format continues throughout the round, with the winning team being selected once they are up more holes than there are holes remaining in the match. Each match is worth 1 total point for the LTD Cup Competition, and matches can end in a draw - in which case both teams will earn half a point.
Sequence of play: The tee box is held by the team who most recently won a hole. In four-ball, partners may play in the order they consider best for their team. So if it is Ted's turn in play order, he can elect to have his teammate Rusty play his shot out of turn if they think it would help the team with decision-making for Ted's shot - either by reading what the shot does or for general tactical knowledge (whether Ted should be aggressive or conservative, etc). Important exception: in TEAM match play if a stroke has been conceded by the other team, the player MUST NOT make the stroke. If the player DOES make the stroke, that player's score for the hole (whether or not the stroke made or missed, the concession counts as holed) stands without penalty. However, the team CAN NO LONGER USE THEIR PARTNER'S SCORE FOR THE HOLE. Example: Ted is lying 3 on the green from about 4 feet away, while Rusty is lying 5 on the green from about 9 feet away, but on a similar line to Ted. Kirk can concede Rusty's putt to keep Ted from learning anything about the break or speed of the putt. But if Rusty elects to strike the putt anyway after the concession, Ted and Rusty will score a 6 for the hole - based upon Kirk's concession of Rusty's 6th stroke - no matter what Ted does with his 4-footer.
Important Notes:
- It is illegal to stand behind your partner while they make a stroke to gain information for your next shot - penalty is loss of hole in match play.
- It is similarly illegal to INTENTIONALLY play your ball to a spot behind your partner to provide them with more information for their ensuing shot - penalty is loss of hole in match play.
- Partners CAN share clubs and equipment - but still must not carry more than 14 clubs in their own bag.
- Partners CAN mark, lift, replace, and drop one another's ball, and can provide advice as though they were one another's caddy.
- Though in normal play you are allowed to take practice putts after holing your shot, note that this is ILLEGAL until your partner has also finished play on the hole - penalty is loss of hole in match play.

Round 2 - Alternate Shot
Format: Alternate Shot - also known as Foursomes - is a 2-man team event where the team plays one ball on each hole and alternates shots as they go - so if Kirk hits the tee shot on hole 1, Conway is stuck hitting the second shot of the hole out of the woods. Kirk then has the third shot, Conway the fourth, etc, until the ball is holed. Once the team moves to the next hole, the order resets, with whomever did NOT tee off on the first hole teeing off on the second hole, even if they are the golfer who made the holing stroke on the previous hole. Since one ball is in play, the 2-man team will be assigned a single handicap made up of 60% of the lower-index player's handicap and 40% of the higher-index player's handicap - so for our exemplary Kirk (17.6) and Kevin (6.1) pairing, we would take 60% of Kevin's 6.1 (3.7) and add it to 40% of Kirk's 17.6 (7.0) for a team handicap of 10.7. At Black Horse off the White Tees (69.4 Course Rating, 125 Slope, Par 72), this would equate to a Course Handicap of 9 for the team. Once again this is a match play event, so the two teams will compare their net scores to determine who wins each hole, with the match being won once a team is ahead by more holes than there are remaining in the match. Each match is worth 1 total point for the LTD Cup Tournament, and matches can end in a draw - in which case both teams will earn half a point. IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION FROM THE LIVE DRAFT: players alternate shots every time, no matter the penalty. So if Kirk hits one out of bounds, Conway hits the next attempt from the very same spot after the penalty stroke. If Kirk hits one into the water, Conway hits the next shot - either from the same spot or from the drop / relief location. If Kirk swings and misses - but intended to hit the ball - it counts as a stroke and Kevin is now up for the next shot from the same location.
Golf Ball: What golf ball do you use? We will NOT be using the one-ball rule, so the team can alternate which ball they use on each hole. Once you start play on a hole, however, you need to continue using the make and model of golf ball you started the hole with. So if you tee off on a hole with a Titleist ProV1 and it gets lost in a creek, out of bounds, or in plain sight, you must replace the ball with another Titleist ProV1. Penalty for changing ball manufacturer or model within a hole is the loss of that hole in match play.
Important Notes:
- It is illegal to stand behind your partner while they make a stroke to gain information for your next shot - penalty is loss of hole in match play.
- DO NOT "finish up" a short one by playing out of order and tapping it in - the penalty for playing out of order is loss of hole in match play.
- Partners CAN share clubs and equipment - but still must not carry more than 14 clubs in their own bag.
- Partners CAN mark, lift, replace, and drop one another's ball, and can provide advice as though they were one another's caddy.

Round 3 - Individual Match
Format: This is it, the ultimate decider of the LTD Cup. Even if one team manages to sweep both of the first two days' matches, 8 points are up for grabs on Sunday and anything can happen! Individual match play is just that - one-on-one match play with full handicap allowance. That handicap allowance is given in the form of the difference in course handicaps for that day. So if Ted (11.4) is playing the White Tees at Bayonet (69.7 Course Rating, 132 Slope, Par 72) his course handicap for the day is 11. Kevin's 6.1 is only good for a Course Handicap of 5, so Kevin is giving Ted 6 strokes (11 - 5 = 6) for the match - which Ted will receive on the 6 hardest holes on the course, handicaps 1 through 6. Generally speaking, match play is pretty straightforward - lower net score wins the hole, each hole is an individual competition and no holes "carry over," and the match is over once one player is ahead by more holes than there are remaining in the match. While the overall LTD Cup will not end in a tie, individual matches CAN be halved - so if the two players finish 18 all square, each team gets half a point toward the LTD Cup. That being said, there are some interesting differences between Match and Stroke play with regard to penalties, playing order, concessions, and where you tee off.

Important Notes:
- The General Penalty - if you play the wrong ball, touch the sand in a bunker with your club (yes Kirk, this is still not allowed), give or ask for advice ("I used a 6 iron"), you typically are assessed a 2-stroke penalty in stroke play. In match play, you lose the hole you are on. 
- Concessions - if a stroke or hole is conceded ("that putt is good," or "I'm so far out of this hole it doesn't matter"), it is considered final - concessions can neither be withdrawn nor declined. If the player makes a putt attempt after a concession and misses, the concession still stands and the player cards the score as though the putt attempt was never made.
- Playing Order is strict - whomever is furthest away from the pin is up in turn order. There is no penalty for playing out of order, HOWEVER if you play out of order and your opponent did not specifically ask you to play out of order, your opponent CAN cancel your stroke and force you to replay it when it is your turn (though they are not required to and can let your stroke stand if they so desire).
- Playing from outside the teeing area - If you tee it up from the wrong tees, outside the tee box markers, or in front of the tee box markers, there is no penalty in match play. However like the playing order rule before, your opponent can choose to cancel your stroke and ask you to play again from inside the correct teeing area. Again, they are absolutely allowed to let your shot stand if they like the trouble you got yourself into.
